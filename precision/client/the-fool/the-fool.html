Shannon Cartier Lucy's paintings are loquacious despite seeming unassuming. They thrust you towards the edge of a precipice right where your inhibitions end and your subconscious begins. Each painting is a clue in a grander narrative arc, but there is neither a linear sequence to the story, nor is there a plot.
The scene is set in the moments preceding your awaking. The hushed tones and the mostly desaturated color palette render the surroundings slightly lugubrious.
The work exists in a liminal space toeing the line between eerie and hilarous. Shannon Cartier Lucy is summoning the subconscious mind to the forefront. In doing so, she reconfigures the world into an unsolvable Rubik's cube.
Lara Atallah

Shannon Cartier Lucy (b. 1977, Nashville, Tennessee) lives and works in Nashville.
She studied at New York University where she received a Bachelor of Arts degree followed by a Master of Science degree from the University of Tennessee.
Recent solo exhibitions include Lubov, New York (forthcoming); Cake on the Floor at Soft Opening, London (2021); Fooled Again at Galerie Hussenot, Paris (2020); The Ever-Flashing Strap at Nina Johnson, Miami (2020); Woman with a Machete at De Boer Gallery, Los Angeles (2020) and Home is a crossword puzzle I can't solve at Lubov, New York (2020). Recent group exhibitions include Bodywork, Discomfort and Existence at Massimo de Carlo, Milan (2021); Nothing but (Flowers) at Karma, New York (2020) and Eigenheim at Soft Opening London (2020).
